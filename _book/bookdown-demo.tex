\chapter*{Overview}\label{overview}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Overview}

This codebook documents variables from three studies --- \textbf{POWERED}, \textbf{MAXED}, and \textbf{MR21} --- grouped into six conceptual categories, with a seventh \emph{Administrative / Other} appendix for REDCap system fields (timestamps, record IDs, form completion flags, consent/signature fields, etc.). Each variable is tagged with the project(s) in which it appears:

Each chapter below has one searchable, sortable, filterable table. Use the column filters at the top of each table to narrow by Measure or Project.

\chapter{Demographics}\label{demographics}

Age, sex assigned at birth, gender identity, pronouns, race/ethnicity, education, household characteristics, and other participant background variables.

\chapter{Anthropometric Measures}\label{anthropometric-measures}

Height, weight, BMI, Tanner staging, and pubertal development indicators.

\chapter{Medical History}\label{medical-history}

Medical diagnoses, surgeries, hospitalizations, medications, menstrual history, substance use, vitals, and prior weight history.

\chapter{Mental Health Diagnosis}\label{mental-health-diagnosis}

Diagnostic interviews and standardized symptom measures --- DIAMOND, BDI-II, PHQ-9, GAD-7, RCADS, YBOCS, OCI, Columbia SSRS, DSM-5 Cross-Cutting Symptoms, MMSE.

\chapter{Eating Disorder Behaviors}\label{eating-disorder-behaviors}

EDE, EPSI, ED100K / ED100K+, ED History, Drive for Thinness/Muscularity, Compulsive Exercise, Exercise Dependence, ART, Goldfarb Fear of Fat, Analogous Exercise Decisions, SRHI (habitual eating), post-interview target-weight survey, and binge / purge / restrict / food-intake / milkshake task items from the visit forms.

\chapter{Socio-behavioral}\label{socio-behavioral}

Anxiety, mood, emotion regulation, perfectionism, social evaluation, body image state, interoception, and other transdiagnostic trait / state measures --- ASI, BIS/BAS, DERS, Frost MPS, IUS, Self-Compassion, SATAQ, BFNE, Perceptions of Teasing, SPSRQ, FADE/FAMB, NVS, PVS, EMA, OCYC Maladaptive, Body Appreciation.

\chapter*{Administrative / Other}\label{administrative-other}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Administrative / Other}

REDCap system fields and study-flow variables: survey timestamps, record IDs, form-complete flags, consent/signature fields, visit start/end times, payment records, and interviewer notes. Included for completeness; not substantive measures.

\chapter*{Notes \& caveats}\label{notes-caveats}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Notes \& caveats}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Mixed intake forms} (DCF, DCF-Parents, Study Visit A/B/C Collection Forms) are split heuristically; review entries flagged in \emph{Administrative / Other} to confirm none should be promoted to a substantive category.
\item
  \textbf{Duplicate variable names} across projects are collapsed into a single row with all source projects listed as badges. If the same name refers to different constructs in different projects, split them in \texttt{codebook.csv} before rendering.
\item
  \textbf{Value coding} is reproduced from the source REDCap export and truncated to 180 chars in the category tables --- full strings are preserved in \texttt{codebook.csv}.
\item
  \textbf{ART (Athletes' Relationship with Training)} is placed under \emph{Eating Disorder Behaviors} because of its use in assessing compulsive exercise in ED populations. Move it to \emph{Socio-behavioral} if you prefer a behavioral-trait framing.
\item
  \textbf{ASI} is treated as the \emph{Anxiety Sensitivity Index} (socio-behavioral), not an eating-symptom inventory.
\item
  \textbf{Tanner Stages / PDS} are treated as anthropometric/maturation indicators. Move them in \texttt{codebook.csv} if your analyses treat them as medical history.
\end{itemize}

To refine categorization, edit \texttt{codebook.csv} (hand-correct the \texttt{category} column) and re-render.
