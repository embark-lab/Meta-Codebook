Eating disorder pathology was assessed using the Eating Disorder Examination (EDE; Fairburn & Cooper, 1993), a structured interview that assesses the frequency and severity of eating disorder symptoms over the past three months. Four subscales are present in the measure (Restraint, Eating Concern, Shape Concern, and Weight Concern) as well as a global score that is the mean of the four subscales. The EDE has been shown to have good reliability and validity in both clinical and non-clinical samples. Test–retest reliability coefficients for the four subscale scores ranged from 0.50 to 0.88 (Berg et al., 2012).

### Subscales

- **Restraint** — attempts to limit food intake
- **Eating Concern** — preoccupation with eating, food, or calories
- **Shape Concern** — dissatisfaction with body shape
- **Weight Concern** — preoccupation with weight and desire to lose weight



### Scoring

Most items are rated on a 0–6 scale. Subscale scores are the mean of their items;
the global score is the mean of the four subscales. Higher scores indicate
greater pathology.

## Key Variables 
`EDE_global` — global score of the EDE, mean of the four subscales
`EDE_restraint_mean` — mean of the restraint subscale items
`EDE_eating_concern_mean` — mean of the eating concern subscale items
`EDE_shape_concern_mean` — mean of the shape concern subscale items
`EDE_weight_concern_mean` — mean of the weight concern subscale items
