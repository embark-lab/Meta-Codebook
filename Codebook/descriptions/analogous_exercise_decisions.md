The **Analogous Exercise Decisions** is a lab made measure is a 21 item self-report questionnaire. The measure assesses participants' preferences between exercise and alternative rewards, including monetary values, caloric amounts, and snack portion sizes.

##Scoring###
Breakpoints were determined in four categories. In the first category, participants completed 10 items comparing increasing monetary values against 30 minutes of exercise. The breakpoint was defined as the point at which the participant first chose money over exercise. The second category followed the same procedure with 10 items comparing increasing monetary values against 1 hour of exercise, with the breakpoint again identified as the first choice of money over exercise. In the third category, participants completed 5 items in which increasing caloric amounts were presented in the context of how much they would be willing to consume in order to engage in 30 minutes of exercise. The breakpoint was defined as the point at which the participant first indicated they would no longer be willing to consume the specified caloric amount to exercise. The final category included 6 items assessing increasing snack portion sizes using the same format, with the breakpoint identified as the point at which the participant first indicated they would not consume the specified amount in order to engage in 30 minutes of exercise.
